History of Microbiology

From spontaneous generation to germ theory

Key Points

1
Antonie van Leeuwenhoek (1632-1723)

Father of Microbiology, first to observe bacteria using homemade microscopes, called them "animalcules".

2
Louis Pasteur (1822-1895)

Disproved spontaneous generation with swan-neck flask experiment. Developed pasteurization and rabies vaccine.

3
Robert Koch (1843-1910)

Established Koch's postulates to prove germ theory. Discovered causative agents of tuberculosis and cholera.

Classification of Microbes

Organizing the tiny world

Key Points

1
Prokaryotes vs Eukaryotes

Prokaryotes (bacteria) lack nucleus and membrane-bound organelles. Eukaryotes (fungi, protozoa) have them.

2
Four Major Groups

Bacteria (prokaryotic), Fungi (eukaryotic), Viruses (non-living particles), Parasites (eukaryotic, unicellular/multicellular).

3
Bacterial Shapes

Cocci (spherical), Bacilli (rod-shaped), Spirilla (spiral-shaped). Arrangements: chains, clusters, pairs.

Funny Memory Tips

Bacterial Cell Structure

What's inside those tiny organisms

Key Points

1
Cell Wall

Made of peptidoglycan. Gram-positive: thick layer. Gram-negative: thin layer + outer membrane with LPS (endotoxin).

2
Capsule

Polysaccharide layer outside cell wall. Protects from phagocytosis and helps adhesion. Like a bacterial "invisibility cloak".

3
Flagella & Pili

Flagella for movement (like bacterial motors). Pili for attachment and genetic exchange (bacterial "dating app").

Funny Memory Tips

Gram-Positive vs Gram-Negative
"Gram-positive bacteria wear thick purple sweaters (peptidoglycan). Gram-negatives wear thin purple T-shirts under a toxic raincoat (LPS outer membrane)!"

"Think of capsule as a king's royal cloak that protects from enemy attacks (phagocytes). No cloak = easier to catch!"

"Flagella are like tiny outboard motors. Monotrichous = one motorboat, Lophotrichous = speedboat with many motors, Peritrichous = all-terrain vehicle with motors everywhere!"

Bacterial Pathogenesis

How bacteria cause disease

Key Points

1
Virulence Factors

Features that help bacteria cause disease: adhesins (stick to host), capsules (avoid phagocytosis), toxins (damage host).

2
Exotoxins vs Endotoxins

Exotoxins: proteins secreted by bacteria (both Gram+ and Gram-). Endotoxins: LPS in Gram-negative cell walls (released when bacteria die).

3
Portals of Entry

Mucous membranes (respiratory, GI, GU tracts), skin (through cuts), parenteral (direct deposition into tissues).
